КЛЮЧЕВАЯ КОНЦЕПЦИЯ КАТЕГОРИИ
Самый важный принцип: каждое видео должно создавать моменты где РОДИТЕЛЬ говорит с ребёнком. Видео задаёт вопрос → пауза → ребёнок и родитель обсуждают → видео отвечает. Это делает контент "совместным" — родители видят, что ребёнок учится через взаимодействие.

🔍 СЦЕНАРИЙ 3.1 — GUESS WHO!
Duration: 22 min | Age: 1–3 years
Concept
Silhouette appears — slowly fills in — child guesses before reveal. CLASSIC format — centuries old in children's entertainment. Parents: "My child is thinking and learning!" Pure anticipation + satisfaction loop.
Format per animal (90 seconds):
1. Black silhouette appears (10 sec) — "Who is this?"
2. Music builds slightly (10 sec) — thinking time
3. First hint reveals (10 sec) — one color/feature appears
4. Second hint (10 sec) — another feature
5. REVEAL (5 sec) — full color reveal + name
6. Animal does characteristic action (30 sec) — reward
7. Narrator confirmation + transition (15 sec)
Structure (15 animals × 90 sec = 22.5 min)
ROUND 1 — Easy animals (familiar)
Animal 1: CAT
* Silhouette: pointy ears, tail, sitting shape
* Hint 1: orange color fills in
* Hint 2: whiskers appear

* Narrator: "It's a CAT! Did you guess it? MEOW!"

Animal 4: RABBIT

👁️ СЦЕНАРИЙ 3.2 — FIND THE DIFFERENCE
Duration: 20 min | Age: 2–4 years
Concept
Two almost identical scenes side by side — one difference. Very slow reveal — child has time to look and find. Builds visual attention, comparison skills, concentration. Parents: "My child is really focused!"
Format per puzzle (90 seconds):
1. Both scenes appear simultaneously (20 sec) — looking time
2. Narrator: "Can you find the difference?" — music plays
3. Animated "eye" sweeps both scenes (15 sec) — hint gesture
4. More looking time (20 sec)
5. Difference highlighted with circle (5 sec) — reveal
6. Celebration (10 sec) — well done!
7. Brief explanation (20 sec) — what was different
Puzzles (14 × 90 sec = 21 min)
EASY PUZZLES (1-5)
Puzzle 1: Two apples
* Left: red apple with green leaf
* Right: red apple with yellow leaf
* Difference: leaf color (easy — color)
* Celebration: apple bounces happily
Puzzle 2: Two cats
* Left: orange cat sitting, tail curled right
* Right: orange cat sitting, tail curled left
* Difference: tail direction (easy — mirror)
Puzzle 3: Two houses
* Left: blue house with red door, 2 windows
* Right: blue house with red door, 3 windows
* Difference: window count (counting)
Puzzle 4: Two butterflies
* Left: purple butterfly, 4 dots on wings
* Right: purple butterfly, 5 dots on wings
* Difference: dot count (counting)
Puzzle 5: Two suns
* Left: yellow sun with 8 rays
* Right: yellow sun with 7 rays
* Difference: ray count

❓ СЦЕНАРИЙ 3.3 — ODD ONE OUT (Что лишнее?)
Duration: 20 min | Age: 2–4 years
Concept
4 items shown — 3 belong together — 1 doesn't fit. Teaches categorization, logic, grouping. Multiple types of "odd one out" — color, shape, category, size. Parents: "This is teaching my child to think logically!"
Format per puzzle (75 seconds):
1. 4 items appear one by one (20 sec)
2. Narrator names each one (10 sec)
3. "Which one doesn't belong?" (20 sec) — thinking time
4. Wrong item wobbles slightly (5 sec) — gentle hint
5. REVEAL — wrong item marked with X, others celebrate (15 sec)
6. Explanation (5 sec) — "Because..."
Puzzle Types
TYPE A — COLOR ODD ONE OUT
Puzzle 1:
* RED apple, RED strawberry, RED rose, BLUE blueberry
* Answer: blueberry (wrong color)
* Explanation: "The blueberry is blue! All the others are red!"
Puzzle 2:
* YELLOW banana, YELLOW sun, YELLOW star, GREEN frog
* Answer: frog (wrong color)
Puzzle 3:
* BLUE sky, BLUE ocean, BLUE butterfly, ORANGE tiger
* Answer: tiger (wrong color)
TYPE B — CATEGORY ODD ONE OUT
Puzzle 4: ANIMALS vs FOOD
* Dog, cat, apple, rabbit
* Answer: apple (not an animal)
* "An apple is food! The others are animals!"
Puzzle 5: VEHICLES vs ANIMAL
* Car, bus, train, elephant
* Answer: elephant (not a vehicle)
Puzzle 6: FRUITS vs VEGETABLE
* Apple, banana, carrot, mango
* Answer: carrot (vegetable, not fruit)
Puzzle 7: ROUND THINGS vs SQUARE
* Ball, orange, clock, box
* Answer: box (square, not round)
Puzzle 8: THINGS THAT FLY vs NOT
* Bird, butterfly, airplane, fish
* Answer: fish (can't fly)
TYPE C — SIZE ODD ONE OUT
Puzzle 9:
* Big elephant, big hippo, big bear, small mouse
* Answer: mouse (small, others big)
Puzzle 10:
* Tiny ant, tiny bee, tiny ladybug, big elephant
* Answer: elephant (big, others tiny)
TYPE D — HABITAT ODD ONE OUT
Puzzle 11:
* Fish, dolphin, crab, camel
* Answer: camel (desert, not ocean)
Puzzle 12:
* Eagle, owl, parrot, goldfish
* Answer: goldfish (lives in water not sky)
TYPE E — SOUND ODD ONE OUT
Puzzle 13:
* Dog (WOOF), cat (MEOW), cow (MOO), fish (silent blub)
* Answer: fish (can't make sounds above water)
Puzzle 14:
* Piano (music), guitar (music), drums (music), hammer (not music)
* Answer: hammer
Puzzle 15:
* Rain (water sound), river (water), ocean (water), fire (crackling)
* Answer: fire (not water)
________________


CELEBRATION SYSTEM
When child is right (parent taps something or video plays out):
* Stars burst from correct items
* Happy music sting
* All 3 correct items do a happy dance
* Narrator: "YES! That's right! [item] doesn't belong because..."
When wrong item is hinted:
* Wrong item wobbles gently
* Soft "hmm" sound — not buzzer (never punishing)
* Narrator: "Look carefully... which one is different?"

🙌 СЦЕНАРИЙ 3.4 — SHOW ME! (Покажи мне!)
Duration: 20 min | Age: 1–2 years
Concept
Voice asks child to point to or touch body part / object. SIMPLEST interactive format — even 12-month babies can do this. Parent co-viewing is essential — they help child understand and respond. Builds receptive language — child understands before they can speak.
Format per round (40 seconds):
1. Question with visual (10 sec) — "Where is your nose?"
2. Character demonstrates (10 sec) — touches own nose
3. Pause — waiting for child (10 sec)
4. Celebration (10 sec) — "YAY! That's your nose!"

REMOTION NOTES — CATEGORY 3
Key components:
* SilhouetteReveal — shape in black gradually fills with color
* SpotlightCircle — animated circle highlighting a difference/answer
* FourItemGrid — 2×2 layout for odd one out puzzles
* ThinkingTimer — subtle visual indicator that thinking time is happening
* CelebrationBurst — confetti + stars on correct answer
* BodyPartArrow — animated bouncing arrow pointing to body part
* FamilyCharacters — parent/child/grandparent characters
Silhouette reveal:
// Phase 1: solid black shape
// Phase 2: color fills in from one side
const fillProgress = interpolate(frame, [hintFrame, hintFrame + 30], [0, 1], {
  extrapolateRight: 'clamp'
});
// Use clipPath that expands to reveal colored version beneath
const clipWidth = fillProgress * characterWidth;


Thinking timer (subtle):
// Small dots at bottom pulsing during thinking time
// NOT a countdown — just ambient "it's okay to think"
const dotPulse = (dotIndex) => 
  Math.sin(frame * 0.15 + dotIndex * 0.8) * 0.3 + 0.7;
